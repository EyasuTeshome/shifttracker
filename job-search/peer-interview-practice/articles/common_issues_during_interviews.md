# Common issues students face during technical job interviews

During technical job interviews, you will experience quite a few stressful moments. To help prepare you for this, we have collected a list of common issues that our job searchers have faced.

They are divided into three groups - each group is connected with a different part of a technical job interview.

Remember that the issues listed below might happen to everybody - especially during such a stressful situation as a job interview. If that happens to you, it does not mean that you are will be rejected. What is more - you can even take an advantage of them!

## Job interview: live coding session - common issues

- You start coding immediately without explaining your plan first and your interviewer has no idea what your plan is.
- You remember that there was a function/method that should do the thing you are supposed to do but you cannot remember its name.
- You need to find a bug or your code should work but it doesn't, so you need to debug it.
- You understood the requirements wrongly and realized the mistake only at the end when the output of your code was not the expected output.
- You have difficulties explaining your thought process.
- You are asked to use a tool that you have never used before and you are a bit lost.
- You are stuck but you are too afraid to ask additional questions.
- You feel in rush and you already know that you won’t implement the solution in a reasonable time. OR: You believe you can implement the solution correctly, but not in the limited time available.
- You implemented a solution that you feel is a good one, but the interviewer asks you how you could refactor it to improve its code quality or performance and you draw a blank.

## Job interview: technical questions - common issues

- You start answering the question, but you think that interviewer's behavior indicates that you said something wrong.
- You have no idea how to answer a specific question.
- You do not understand the question or it takes you a significant amount of time to understand it.
- You do not remember the details, you just recall the general concept or you are having problems with articulating your understanding of the concept.
- You are getting so nervous that you feel like your mind is empty - even if you know that you knew the answer.
- You thought you knew the answer, but you received feedback at the end that one or more answers you gave confidently were actually incorrect or partially incorrect.

## Job interview: talking about your project

- You can see that interviewer does not follow your presentation.
- While presenting your app something does not work.
- You do not feel confident enough to talk about your project.
- You are over-explaining things.
- You have a constant feeling that your code from the past is not good enough.
- You forget to provide the not-technical context of the project: working with the team, the purpose of the project etc.
- You talk so much about the impact you made on a project that it begins to feel like you’re exaggerating things.
