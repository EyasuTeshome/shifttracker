# Peer-to-peer live coding practice: abrupt coders


## Learning objectives

- Demonstrate ability to deal with common issues during the live coding session part of a technical interview.

### Estimated time: 1h

## Exercise

IMPORTANT NOTE: This exercise purpose is to practice talking about your plan for coding not coding itself. So, remember - **do not write any code** during this interview practice.

### Instructions

- Join the Zoom call with your team - remember to always be on time to respect your teammates’ time and practice respecting your interviewers’ time.
- Open the [meeting outline](https://github.com/microverseinc/curriculum-professional-skills/blob/main/job-search/peer-interview-practice/articles/peer_common_issues_practice_meeting_outline.md).
- **Special instructions**
    
    *Follow the steps listed in the meeting outline with your team with the following adjustments.*
    
    `Preparation`
    
    - Read the theory lesson [Deal with common issues during live coding: abrupt coders](https://github.com/microverseinc/curriculum-professional-skills/blob/main/job-search/peer-interview-practice/explain_first_lesson.md) lesson.
    
     `Interviewers setup` 
    
    - Everybody opens [this spreadsheet](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1HkUyBZdcpGz_aEUa8W_rtNhS739jly8HY6sXVPPSAro/edit#gid=2041017957) and gets a randomly generated set of coding challenges in the 3rd row.
    - Everybody reads the selected challenge - as all of you will be playing the interviewer role you need to get familiar with the challenge.
    
     `Quick interviews` 
    
    - As an interviewer you should send a link to the coding challenge that you got familiar with to your applicant.
    - As an applicant, you will need a couple of minutes or reading the challenge - take your time here.
    - As an interviewer, you should use the [following rubric for giving advice](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1aJDc4X45WmoftWhmDyPafq9ZcXPWBH8av6KhDdGfYpo/edit#).
    
    `Recap`
    
    - Remember to fill-in this [airtable form](https://airtable.com/shrclyLFtL6b5fMdT).

### Submit your exercise

Paste the link to the rubric with advice that was filled in by your interviewer (thanks to that you will be able to come back to them at any point) from your dashboard.
